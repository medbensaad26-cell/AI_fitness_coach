# WHO Global Recommendations on Physical Activity for Health

## Adults (18–64 years)
- **Aerobic**: 150–300 min of moderate‑intensity, or 75–150 min of vigorous‑intensity aerobic activity per week, or an equivalent combination.
- **Muscle strengthening**: ≥2 days/week of moderate or greater intensity involving all major muscle groups.
- **Additional benefits**: Exceeding 300 min moderate or 150 min vigorous further reduces health risks.
- **Sedentary time**: Limit; replacing sedentary time with any intensity of physical activity yields health benefits.

## Older Adults (65+ years)
- Same as adults, plus:
  - Multicomponent physical activity (balance, strength, endurance) ≥3 days/week to prevent falls.
  - Tailor to individual capabilities and health conditions.

## Children & Adolescents (5–17 years)
- ≥60 min/day of moderate‑to‑vigorous physical activity, mostly aerobic.
- Vigorous‑intensity and muscle‑bone strengthening activities ≥3 days/week.

## Pregnant & Postpartum Women
- ≥150 min of moderate‑intensity aerobic activity per week, if no contraindications.
- Include muscle strengthening; avoid activities with high risk of falling or abdominal trauma.

## Key Messages
- Any physical activity is better than none. Start with small amounts and gradually increase.
- Muscle‑strengthening benefits are independent of aerobic activity.
- Reducing sedentary time is crucial; interrupt prolonged sitting with light activity.
