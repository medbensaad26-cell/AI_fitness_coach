# ISSN Position Stand on Protein and Exercise (Key Points)

## Daily Protein Intake
- For building and maintaining muscle mass, an overall daily protein intake in the range of **1.4–2.0 g/kg body weight/day** is recommended for physically active individuals.
- Higher intakes (2.3–3.1 g/kg of fat‑free mass) may be required during energy restriction to preserve lean mass.

## Per‑Meal Dose
- Muscle protein synthesis (MPS) is maximally stimulated with **0.25–0.40 g/kg per meal**, which equates to 20–40 g of high‑quality protein.
- Spreading protein intake across 3–6 meals per day (every 3–4 hours) sustains MPS throughout the day.

## Quality & Source
- Complete proteins (containing all essential amino acids, particularly leucine ~2–3 g per meal) are superior for stimulating MPS.
- Whey protein is rapidly digested and rich in leucine, making it an effective post‑exercise protein source.
- Plant‑based proteins can also support muscle anabolism when consumed in sufficient doses and combined to balance amino acid profiles.

## Timing
- Pre‑sleep protein (~30–40 g casein) can increase overnight MPS and improve recovery.
- Post‑exercise protein ingestion (within 2 hours) is recommended but the anabolic window is wider than previously thought; total daily intake is the most important factor.

## Safety
- Protein intakes up to 3.5 g/kg/day are generally safe for healthy individuals; there is no evidence linking high protein diets to kidney damage in healthy populations.
