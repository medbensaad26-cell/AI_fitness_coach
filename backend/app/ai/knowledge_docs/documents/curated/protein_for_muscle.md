# Protein Intake for Muscle Hypertrophy and Repair

## Daily Requirements
- **Sedentary adults**: 0.8 g/kg body mass.
- **General fitness**: 1.2–1.6 g/kg.
- **Strength/hypertrophy**: 1.6–2.2 g/kg (ISSN); doses up to 2.4 g/kg may benefit during caloric deficit.
- **Endurance athletes**: 1.2–1.4 g/kg; higher during intense training blocks.

## Per‑Meal Dosing
- Maximal muscle protein synthesis (MPS) stimulation occurs with 0.4 g/kg/meal (≈20–40 g high‑quality protein).
- Consuming protein beyond ~0.6 g/kg/meal does not further increase MPS but may aid satiety.
- Distribute protein across 3–5 meals/day (every 3–4 h) to sustain MPS peaks.

## Timing Around Exercise
- **Pre‑workout**: 20–30 g 1–3 h before training can reduce muscle damage and improve recovery.
- **Post‑workout**: The "anabolic window" is wider than once believed. Consume 20–40 g within 2 h after training; total daily intake is more critical.
- **Before sleep**: 30–40 g casein (slow‑digesting) overnight can support overnight MPS and recovery.

## Protein Quality & Sources
- **Complete proteins** (all essential amino acids, high leucine): whey, casein, eggs, poultry, beef, fish, dairy, soy.
- **Plant‑based**: Combine grains and legumes to obtain full amino acid profile (e.g., rice + pea protein). Soy is a standalone complete plant protein.
- **Leucine trigger**: ≥2–3 g leucine per meal is needed to maximally stimulate MPS (found in ~25 g whey, 170 g chicken, or 6 eggs).

## Myths & Facts
- **Myth**: "Excess protein harms kidneys." Fact: In healthy individuals, high protein intake does not damage renal function (Poortmans & Dellalieux, 2000).
- **Myth**: "You must drink a shake immediately post‑workout." Fact: Total daily intake and overall energy balance matter more.
- **Myth**: "Plant protein is inferior." Fact: When dosed appropriately and combined, plant proteins can be equally effective.

## Practical Recommendations for Coaches
- Calculate daily target: body weight (kg) × 1.6–2.2 g.
- Advise clients to include a protein source at each meal.
- For convenience, whey or plant protein supplements are an effective tool, not a requirement.
