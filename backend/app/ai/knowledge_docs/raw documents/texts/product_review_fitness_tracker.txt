Review: FitPro X200 Fitness Tracker – 3 Month Honest Review

Rating: 3/5 stars

I bought the FitPro X200 back in January after seeing a ton of ads for it on Instagram. It's marketed as a budget-friendly alternative to the Apple Watch, with heart rate monitoring, sleep tracking, GPS, and 50+ workout modes. At $79, it seemed like a steal.

The Good:
- Battery life is excellent – I get about 10 days on a single charge.
- The screen is bright and readable outdoors.
- Step count seems accurate, matching my phone's pedometer.
- The app is decent, though a bit cluttered with ads for premium features.

The Bad:
- Heart rate tracking is wildly inaccurate during workouts. It often shows my HR at 90 bpm when I'm dripping sweat and my chest strap reads 150+.
- Sleep tracking is hit or miss. It counts me as "asleep" when I'm lying in bed reading.
- The GPS takes forever to lock onto a signal – sometimes 2-3 minutes.
- The band gave me a rash after a few weeks (I don't have sensitive skin).

The Verdict:
It's okay for step counting and casual use, but if you're serious about training, spend a bit more on a Garmin or Polar. The heart rate data is just not reliable enough for zone training. I'll be returning mine and upgrading.

Would I recommend? Only for a complete beginner who just wants to track steps.
