Tech Neck: How Your Phone Is Wrecking Your Posture (And How to Fix It)

By Dr. Lisa Park, Chiropractor

We spend hours hunched over our phones and laptops. The result? "Tech neck" – a condition where the head juts forward, putting massive strain on the cervical spine. For every inch your head moves forward, the weight on your neck increases by about 10 pounds. Over time, this can lead to chronic pain, headaches, and even disc issues.

Signs you have tech neck:
- Rounded shoulders
- Forward head posture (your ears are in front of your shoulders)
- Upper back and neck pain that gets worse as the day goes on
- Frequent tension headaches
- Tingling or numbness in your arms

The good news: it's reversible. Here's a simple routine to do daily:

1. Chin Tucks: Sit tall, pull your chin straight back (like making a double chin). Hold 5 seconds, release. Do 10 reps, 3x/day.
2. Doorway Chest Stretch: Stand in a doorway with arms at 90° against the frame. Lean forward until you feel a stretch in your chest. Hold 30 seconds.
3. Thoracic Spine Extension: Lie on a foam roller placed under your mid-back, support your head, and gently arch back. 10 slow breaths.
4. Wall Angels: Stand with your back against a wall, arms at 90° (like a goalpost). Slowly slide your arms up and down the wall. 10 reps.
5. Bruegger's Relief Position: Sit at the edge of a chair, palms up, chest open, look up slightly. Hold 30 seconds.

Combine these with ergonomic adjustments: raise your screen to eye level, take frequent breaks, and consider a standing desk. Your neck will thank you.
